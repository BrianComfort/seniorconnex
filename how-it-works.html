How it works

A quiet system,
always paying attention.

SeniorConnex is a complete care network — a biometric watch on the wrist, a contactless radar sensor in the room, a privacy-shutter camera, and a bedside tablet for one-tap video calls, tied together by HIPAA-compliant software that turns the family, the in-home caregiver, and the facility into one shared view.

What arrives in the box

Four pieces of hardware. One care network.

Every SeniorConnex kit ships pre-provisioned and paired to your network. There is no app for your loved one to install, no Wi-Fi wizard to fight, no QR codes to scan. Plug in the tablet, mount the radar with two screws, slip the watch on the wrist — the first reading streams to every phone in the network in under five minutes.

Biometric watch

Wrist-worn. Heart rate, motion, rest, and fall detection — all day, all night, on a multi-day charge.

Pontosense radar

Wall-mounted mmWave sensor. Reads breathing and presence through clothes and blankets — no camera, no mic.

Privacy-shutter camera

Optional. Physical shutter closed by default — only opens when a video call is active in the room.

Bedside tablet

Big buttons, large type, one tap to call. Designed for the person at the center, not the engineer.

01 · The radar sensor

Continuous biometrics, without anything on the body.

A small mmWave radar mounts on the wall or ceiling. It senses breathing, presence, and motion across the room — through clothes and blankets, in the dark. There is no microphone. There is no camera. The resident forgets it is there.

  • Detects breathing and presence through clothing, sheets, and walls
  • Fuses with the biometric watch to classify a fall from a sit-down
  • No cameras and no audio — privacy preserved by physics, not policy
  • One sensor covers a bedroom; multi-sensor arrays cover larger spaces

02 · The biometric watch

For when the care plan calls for clinical-grade detail.

The biometric watch on the wrist gives the network continuous heart rate, motion, and rest data — and pairs with the radar to make fall detection precise. It is lightweight, water-resistant, and slips onto the wrist like any modern watch.

Continuous heart rate

Resting rate, active rate, and any sustained anomaly — streamed to the dashboard in real time.

Multi-day battery

Magnetic charging dock once every few days. No nightly compliance, no daily-charging nag.

Pairs automatically

Slips onto the wrist, syncs to the room sensor, and disappears into the background.

03 · The app

Calm by default. Loud only when it matters.

Open the app and the first thing you see is reassurance — heart rate, presence, rest — in plain language. The same view is shared with everyone in the care network: the family on a phone, the in-home caregiver on a tablet, the facility on a clinical dashboard.

  • One-screen overview — vitals, presence, trend
  • Per-family permissions, set by the facility or the primary caregiver
  • Available on iOS, Android, and the web
  • Same data, three role-aware views — true transparency across the network

04 · Video calls from the tablet

A grandchild's face, one tap away.

The bedside tablet shows three buttons. Family. Caregiver. Help. One tap and the call rings on every authorized phone in the network. The family member who answers steps into the room. The privacy-shutter camera opens only for the duration of the call, then closes itself.

  • One-tap calling — no menus, no PINs, no contact list to scroll
  • Privacy shutter opens only while the call is connected
  • Family answers by SMS link — no app install required
  • Calls work over Wi-Fi or LTE — your choice at install

05 · Automated alerts

Detect. Classify. Escalate. Document.

When something changes, SeniorConnex does not make you read a chart to find out. The system identifies real events the moment they happen and routes SMS escalation in seconds — to on-shift staff first, and to family on your policy. Tap any step below to see how it works.

Detect — against the person's own baseline

Not a population average. The watch and radar learn what calm looks like for them.

 +
Within the first 48 hours, the system learns the resident's resting heart rate, normal motion patterns, and typical sleep rhythm. Every signal is then evaluated against their personal baseline — so an athlete's 48 bpm and a heart patient's 84 bpm are both correctly understood as normal.

Classify — a fall, a sit, a nap, a real event

Sensor fusion turns raw signal into a high-confidence decision.

 +
The watch detects the spike in motion. The radar confirms the change in vertical posture. Together they distinguish a fall from a sit-down, a cardiac event from a caffeine bump, an unusual stillness from a nap. Fewer false alarms means real alarms get taken seriously.

Escalate — the right person, in seconds

SMS to on-shift staff first. Family second. Two-way acknowledgment.

 +
SMS hits the on-shift caregiver's phone within seconds. They acknowledge with a single tap. The family sees the acknowledgment land on their phone, too, so they know the right person is already on it. If no one acknowledges in 30 seconds, escalation steps to the next person in the chain.

Document — every alert, automatically logged

Encrypted, time-stamped, ready for the care-plan review.

 +
Every alert, every acknowledgment, every resolution is captured in the audit log. The clinical team can run a care-plan review without re-creating the timeline from memory. The family can read the same record their loved one's care team reads.
