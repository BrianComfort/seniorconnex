The quiet worry

You can't be in the room every hour. You shouldn't have to wonder what's happening in the ones you're not.

A calm, glanceable view

The app says all you need to know

Clear and concise information on the state of your loved one the moment you open the SeniorConnex app. Critical information up front, shared with you, your family and caregivers.

  • Live vitals refreshed continuously, in plain language
  • Presence and movement — is Mom up, resting, or asleep?
  • Trend over the last 24 hours, the last week, the last month

When something changes

Immediate SMS — the moment something needs attention.

A fall. A cardiac event. Respiration drifting outside normal range. SeniorConnex sends an SMS to your phone in seconds — and, when they're in a facility, to their care team at the same time. You're never the last to know.

01

Fall detected

Radar identifies a fall in seconds — without cameras, floor sensors, or anything they have to wear.

02

Vitals out of range

Heart rate or respiration drifting outside their personal baseline. Alert before it becomes an emergency.

03

Unusual stillness

Movement stops where it shouldn't — the bathroom floor at 2am, an unexpectedly long stretch in bed.

For them — invisible

Nothing to wear, charge, learn, or remember.

The hardest part of getting an older parent to use a pendant or wristband is the wearing of it. SeniorConnex skips that fight entirely. A small radar sensor mounts on the wall or ceiling. They forget it's there within a week. You never have to remind them, fix it, charge it, or replace its battery.

  • No buttons. No bracelets. No "did you charge it last night?"
  • Works through clothes, sheets, and walls — even in the dark
  • Their privacy preserved — no cameras, no audio

Who SeniorConnex is for

The people doing the worrying.

Adult children

Long-distance caregivers who can't drive over every night. Glance at your phone before bed and sleep through the night.

Spouses

For the partner sleeping next to someone managing a heart condition, dementia or recovery. Sleep deeper. Wake calmer.

Family caregivers

Daughters, sons, in-laws, and siblings. A second set of eyes for the hours you have to be elsewhere.
