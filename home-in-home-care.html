What we sell

A complete care kit and the software that turns it into one network.

SeniorConnex is not just a wearable. It is a complete kit: biometric watch, contactless radar sensor, privacy-first in-room camera, and a bedside tablet. All software is HIPAA-compliant and ties the family, the in-home caregiver, and the facility into one shared view and alert system.

Biometric watch

Continuous heart rate, rest, motion, and fall detection without being intrusive.

Pontosense radar

Wall-mounted mmWave sensor measures position and breathing without a camera in rooms where you want privacy, such as a bedroom.

Room camera

A camera to capture an entire room.

Bedside tablet

One-tap video calls. Big buttons, large type, no menus to navigate. Designed for the person being cared for.

A day on shift

Less to remember. More to notice.

The shift gets simpler when the system carries the chart work. Tap any step below to see what changes.

Arrive — read the room before you enter it

Overnight vitals, rest quality, and any flagged events are already on your screen.

 +
You don't need a briefing. The previous shift's notes, the watch's overnight readings, and any flagged events are already on the tablet when you walk in. You step into the room knowing the state.

Care — be present, not paperwork

Vitals stream from the watch. The radar watches presence. You handle the human work.

+
The biometric watch tracks heart rate, rest, and motion without you taking a single manual measurement. The radar tracks presence and breathing through clothing. Your hands are free for bathing, meals, and conversation.

Connect — bring the family into the room

One tap on the tablet starts a video call with anyone in the network.

 +
For a quick check-in or a tough moment, tap "call" on the tablet. The privacy shutter opens, the family answers, and the call ends when you close it. No app for them to install — they just answer the SMS link on their phone.

Escalate — alerts route by policy

A real fall pages you first. The family hears it second.

 +
When the watch and radar together detect a fall, the SMS hits your phone within seconds. You acknowledge. The family sees the acknowledgment land on their phone, too. No one is in the dark, and no one is panicking without information.

Handoff — the next caregiver is already briefed

Your notes, the timeline, and the vitals are visible to whoever takes over.

 +
End of shift. The notes, observations, and vitals are already in the same view the next caregiver and the family use. No group text needed. No clipboard. Everyone is on the same page when you walk out.

Transparency is the trust

Family, facility, and you on the same page.

The family, the in-home caregiver, and any facility involved see the same data, at the same moment, with the same privacy. No screenshots forwarded. No one left out of the loop. Just one shared view and alert system that everyone can trust.
