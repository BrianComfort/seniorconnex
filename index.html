Bedside tablet

The process

From the box at the door to the first quiet night.

Click any step to read the detail. The whole thing — from unboxing to the first live alert — takes about a day.

Talk to us — we scope the care network

A real human, not a chat widget. About 20 minutes. (tktk FIX THIS PROCESS IN THE MEETING)

 +
We walk you through who needs to be in the care network — adult children, spouses, an in-home caregiver, a facility team. We confirm the rooms, the watches, and the alert escalation policy. You decide who gets paged first and who hears it second.

Kit arrives pre-provisioned

Watch, radar, tablet, and optional camera — all paired to your account.

 +
The kit ships to the address you provide. Every piece is already paired to the care network — no QR codes to scan, no Wi-Fi setup wizards to fight. Plug in the tablet, mount the radar with two screws (or a stick-on plate), and slip the watch on the wrist.

First reading in under five minutes

Heart rate, presence, and the live feed light up on every phone in the network.

 +
The watch streams its first reading within minutes. The radar calibrates to the room's empty baseline. Everyone in the network gets a welcome notification — and the live feed on their phone starts moving.

Alerts go live on day one

Falls, vitals changes, and inactivity flags — routed by your policy.

 +
The system starts watching from minute one. A real fall escalates by SMS in seconds — to the on-shift caregiver first, to family on the policy you set. Two-way acknowledgment confirms the message landed with someone who can act.

We check in. You stay connected.

Weekly during pilot, monthly thereafter. Always a human.

 +
For the first month we check in weekly to tune thresholds, adjust alert routing, and make sure the watch sits comfortably. After that, monthly. The line is never automated — you talk to the same person every time.
